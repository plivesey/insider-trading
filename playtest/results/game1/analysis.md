# Game 1 Analysis — Hearst vs Vanderbilt vs Morgan

## Result
- **Winner: Hearst ($27)** — 2 goals claimed
- 2nd: Morgan ($24) — 0 goals claimed
- 3rd: Vanderbilt ($19) — 0 goals claimed

## Final Prices
Blue $5, Orange $3, Yellow $7, Purple $4

## Game Summary (25 turns, tracker 9/9)

### Early Game (T1-T9): Positioning
- Hearst committed to Blue+Purple from T1, buying Purple blank ($5) then Blue blank ($4)
- Vanderbilt bought Connected Broker ($5) and played it immediately (T5), then Orange stock_up ($5)
- Morgan used Corner the Market to grab Purple stock_up for free (T6)
- T9 was the pivotal bidding war: Vanderbilt spent $10 to block Hearst from completing Blue+Purple two-pair goal

### Mid Game (T10-T18): Insider Tip War
- T10: Hearst claimed "2 Purple" goal (first goal of the game!) — drew insider tip as reward
- T13-T17: Rapid insider tip exchanges destroyed stock values:
  - Hearst: Yellow +2/Purple +1 (T13)
  - Vanderbilt: sold Blue stock_down with broker bonus (T14)
  - Morgan: Purple -3 (T15) — crushed Hearst's Purple holdings
  - Hearst: Orange -3 (T16) — retaliation against Vanderbilt
  - Vanderbilt: Orange +2/Yellow +1 (T17) — recovery
- Vanderbilt sold Yellow insider for $10 (broker bonus) and drew Blue -3 tip (T20)

### End Game (T19-T25): Goal Sprint
- T19: Hearst bought Purple hype cheaply ($4), getting 3rd Purple
- T22: Hearst claimed "3 Purple" goal — adjusted all stocks favorably. Tracker hit 8/9!
- T23: Morgan bought Yellow blank ($6) in desperation
- T24: Morgan played Rumor Mill brilliantly — adjusted prices without advancing tracker, tying Hearst at $27
- T25: Hearst's kill shot — played Yellow -3, crashing Morgan's Yellow stock and ending the game

## Key Observations

### Goals Were Decisive
Hearst was the ONLY player to claim goals (2 total). This validates the playtesting insight that goals are the primary differentiator. Both goal rewards (draw insider tip, adjust all stocks) provided compounding advantages.

### Connected Broker Underperformed
Vanderbilt spent $5 + 1 turn action on Connected Broker but only sold 1 stock the entire game (Yellow insider for +$1 bonus). Net value: ~$1 from a $5 investment. In a short 25-turn game, persistent effects need more time to pay off.

### Insider Tips as Weapons
The mid-game insider tip war (T13-T17) was dramatic but largely zero-sum. The key insight: insider tips are most valuable when timed to either (a) boost your own holdings just before game end, or (b) serve as a kill shot (Hearst's Yellow -3 on the final turn).

### Cash Preservation
Morgan maintained the highest cash throughout but invested too late in stocks. Vanderbilt's aggressive $10 block on T9 left them cash-poor for the rest of the game.

### Color Focus
- Hearst: Blue+Purple (as planned) — claimed 2 goals
- Vanderbilt: Orange+Blue (sold the Blue) — never completed a goal
- Morgan: Purple+Yellow — never completed a goal

### What Each Player Could Have Done Better
- **Morgan**: Should have bought stocks earlier rather than Corner the Market. The action card cost a turn and only yielded a $2 Purple stock via the free take.
- **Vanderbilt**: The $10 block on T9 was too expensive. Should have let Hearst have the Blue and focused on their own Orange accumulation and goal completion.
- **Hearst**: Played nearly optimally. The only weakness was overpaying for Purple ($7 on T7), but the goal claims more than made up for it.

### Game Stats
- 25 turns total (~8-9 per player)
- Tracker advances: 1 crisis card, 2 goal claims, 6 insider tips played = 9 total
- 1 crisis card drawn (T2 market refill), 1 never drawn
- Connected Broker was the only persistent card played
- Action cards seen: Corner the Market (played), Connected Broker (played), Rumor Mill (played), The Squeeze/Preferred Bidder/Wild Speculation (market, unplayed)
- Starting cash $30 each. Final cash: Vanderbilt $13, Morgan $13, Hearst $10

### Design Feedback
- Game length of 25 turns (~8 per player) felt appropriate for 3 players
- The goal system worked well — the 2-goal player won, validating the "goals win games" design intent
- Insider tip timing created genuine tension between playing for advantage vs. advancing the clock
- Connected Broker may be overvalued by AI players — $5 + turn action for +$1/sell needs 5+ sells to break even
- The Rumor Mill not advancing tracker was a critical strategic distinction that Morgan leveraged well
- Purple hype at $2 was correctly identified as undervalued by Hearst — hype stocks at low prices are goal enablers
